Common Questions

Before You Ask

The questions we get most. If yours isn't here, just ask.

Why no solo traveler upcharge?

Because it's unfair and economically unnecessary. Solo travelers are one of the largest and most underserved demographics in group travel — and they get penalized everywhere else. We build our pricing to work with groups of 8–10 without needing to penalize anyone for traveling alone. One price. Everyone pays the same.

How is this different from Gate1 or a big group tour?

Gate1 and similar operators move 30–45 people by bus through as many stops as possible. The model is volume — see a lot, understand none of it, spend half your time in transit. We cap at 10. Every itinerary is built around a single intellectual theme. We engineer social connection. And we eliminate decision fatigue by curating your free time so it's actually free. Less bus. More depth. Real connections.

Do I need to be an academic to enjoy these trips?

Absolutely not. You need to be curious — that's it. Our pre-trip reading lists are designed to bring anyone up to speed regardless of background. You don't need a Spanish Literature degree to be moved by Lorca's Granada. You don't need art training to understand why Las Meninas changed painting forever. You just need context. That's what we provide.

I've done every Barcelona port excursion. Is there really more to see?

There is so much more. The Recinte Modernista de Sant Pau — a UNESCO World Heritage Site ten minutes from Sagrada Família that almost nobody visits. The Born Market underground excavations where an entire neighborhood destroyed in 1714 lies beneath your feet. Torre Bellesguard, Gaudí's medieval castle that most Barcelona residents have never been to. The Bunkers del Carmel — the best view in the city, free, and almost entirely tourist-free. The cruise excursion shows you what everyone sees. We show you what the city actually is.

What is a hammam and why is the Vivir Bien trip built around it?

A hammam is a traditional Moorish bathhouse — warm pool, hot pool, cold plunge, steam, kessa scrub, ghassoul clay mask — a bathing ritual the Arab world brought to Spain and practiced continuously for over a thousand years. The cities of Granada and Córdoba have extraordinary restored hammams that are nothing like a hotel spa. One session at Aire Ancient Baths in Chicago costs $400. The Vivir Bien trip includes two full hammam rituals, ancient Roman-Arab thermal springs, olive oil therapy from the grove that pressed it, and a week in Andalusia — for $3,200. The math speaks for itself.

Can you build a trip around my cruise sailing dates?

Yes — this is one of our specialties. As a licensed travel agent, we work with your existing sailing schedule to build pre or post-cruise land extensions, or full-day shore experiences in port cities. We know Barcelona, Málaga, Cádiz, Cartagena, and the surrounding areas well. If you've done every port stop before and want something genuinely different, we're the right call.

What's in the pre-trip reading list?

Each trip gets a curated PDF delivered 60 days before departure. It includes one book (readable, not academic), two essays, one film, and a playlist — all chosen to put you in the place intellectually and emotionally before you arrive. For the Literary Spain trip: a selection of Lorca's poems, a Civil War essay, Almodóvar's Volver, and a flamenco playlist. For the Art trip: an essay on Velázquez's revolutionary technique, a Goya biography excerpt, and the Prado's own audio guide recommendations. You arrive knowing just enough to be genuinely curious — not overwhelmed.

What is your cancellation policy?

$500 deposit secures your spot and is fully refundable up to 90 days before departure. Between 90–60 days, 50% is refundable. The full balance is due at 60 days and is non-refundable after that date. We strongly recommend travel insurance — as a licensed agent, we can help you find a policy that actually covers what matters. Reach out and we'll walk you through your options.

Can I come on a trip if I don't speak Spanish?

Completely. All our trips are conducted in English. Our local guides are bilingual specialists. The only Spanish you need is por favor, gracias, and a willingness to try the menu del día. That said — if you do speak Spanish, these trips will deepen your connection to the language in ways no classroom ever could. Standing in Granada knowing why Lorca used the specific vocabulary he did is a different experience when you can read it in the original.
