The Information Gap

This is not a talent problem.
It is an information problem.

Organizations have always had more information than employees about what work is worth, what the real criteria for advancement are, and what the ceiling looks like before you hit it. That asymmetry has always worked in the employer's favor.

Visible does not give you their information. It makes sure you have structured, analyzed, and understood your own — completely — before any consequential decision is made.

💰

A cost of living raise is not recognition. It is maintenance. A 3% raise in a 4% inflation year is a pay cut — and most people never see that calculation until it is too late to act on it.

🔍

Your most valuable work is invisible. The contribution that kept everything running. The fragment that made the whole presentation possible. The system you built that IT never did. None of it appears in a standard performance review.

⏱️

Promotions are decided before the conversation. By the time you are in the room, the decision is mostly made. Visible helps you influence it before you get there.

🚪

The ceiling is structural, not personal. When your manager says you have reached the top of your band, they are telling you something specific about the organization — not about your capability.
